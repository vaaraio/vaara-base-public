Henri Sirkkavaara

Founder

Henri Sirkkavaara is the founder of Vaara, an independent signal intelligence venture built for high-stakes decision environments where the quality of information determines outcomes. Vaara was created to address a persistent operational problem across complex systems: too much incoming data, too little effective filtering, and costly decisions made on noise rather than signal.

Before founding Vaara, Henri Sirkkavaara designed and delivered enterprise IT infrastructure across multiple sectors, managed real-time production systems in broadcast media, and worked in information science across two public institutions. Across each of these environments, the underlying challenge was consistent: ensuring that the right information reached the right people at the right time.

For over a decade, Henri Sirkkavaara has also operated in DeFi as a trader, advisor to protocol founders, and builder of systematic frameworks for on-chain decision-making. That experience revealed a structural gap in the market: protocols managing significant capital often lacked the information infrastructure required to separate conviction from noise. Vaara was established to solve that problem with purpose-built signal systems for capital allocators, protocol operators, and high-consequence decision makers.

The core thesis is simple: the system that decides when not to act is often more valuable than the system that acts.

The name Vaara comes from Finnish. It refers to a forested highland and also carries the meaning of danger — a fitting expression of Vaara's mission to rise above noise and improve visibility in complex terrain.
